Research (05/2023)

Digital Note-Taking Affordances Effect on Cognitive Workload

This project focused on the educational technology, we explored the impact of different digital note-taking methods on students' cognitive workload. Our research scrutinized the use of tablets in classrooms, comparing the cognitive effects of note-taking with a stylus, on-screen keyboard, and attached keyboard. Contrary to expectations, our findings indicated that the mode of digital note-taking did not significantly influence cognitive workload. The collaborative nature of this project sharpened my teamwork and research skills. Working alongside my peers, I gained proficiency in the design and execution of a structured study, data collection, and critical analysis. We integrated findings from Segal (2011), which highlighted the benefits of tablets in educational settings, to contextualize our results within the broader landscape of classroom technology. The teamwork aspect taught me the value of diverse perspectives and the strength of collective effort in achieving research goals. This experience has equipped me with a strong foundation in collaborative inquiry and the application of empirical evidence to support academic findings, preparing me for future ventures in the field of educational technology research.

Research Project (01/2023)

Effects of Aesthetics on Usability

This project explored the impact of website aesthetics on usability, engaging 50 participants to analyze two designs via the System Usability Scale (SUS). Findings revealed that minimalism significantly enhances usability, highlighting aesthetics' crucial role in user experience. This venture advanced my skills in UX research, teamwork, and data analysis, reinforcing my dedication to crafting visually appealing, user-friendly interfaces.
